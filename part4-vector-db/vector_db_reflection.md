## Vector DB Use Case

Traditional keyword searches is often a pain taking with legal contracts because they only look for exact matches. `If a lawyer searches for "termination clauses," but the contract says "ending the agreement," the search will fail.`

#A vector database solve uses semantic search, which focuses on the meaning behind words instead of just the spelling. It works by solving:

  -Understanding Meaning: It turns both the contract text and the lawyer's question into embeddings (a list of numbers that represent a concept).

  -Smart Matching: Because it compares concepts, the system understands that "termination" and "cancellation" are essentially the same thing.

  -The Process: The contract is broken into small pieces (like paragraphs). Each piece is converted into an embedding and stored. When a lawyer asks a question, the system finds the pieces that have the most similar meaning.


Thus, a vector database plays a crucial role in enabling intelligent, context-aware search, making it far more effective than traditional keyword-based methods for large and complex legal documents.
